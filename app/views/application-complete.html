What happens next

{{ data['local-council'] }} will contact you within 3 working days to confirm your temporary event notice or ask additional questions.

The council can’t refuse a notice unless the police or environmental health object to it. They must do this within 3 working days of receiving your application. They can only object if they think your event could:

  • lead to crime and disorder
  • cause a public nuisance
  • be a threat to public safety
  • put children at risk of harm

If there’s an objection, your council’s licensing committee will hold a meeting (called a ‘hearing’) no later than 24 hours before the event (unless all parties agree that a hearing isn’t needed).

Appeals

If you disagree with your local committee’s decision, you can appeal to your local magistrates’ court. You must do this within 21 days, and at least 5 working days before the date of your event.

Displaying your notice

You must keep your TEN in a safe place where the event is held.

You must also display a copy of the notice where it can be easily seen.
